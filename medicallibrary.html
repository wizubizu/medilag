Resources

  • The Library’s resources are based on the curriculum of the College. Its collection is made up of hard and soft copies of textbooks, journals, periodicals, audio-visuals, multimedia and robust online resources.
  • There are a number of special collections including CMUL/UNILAG official publications, inaugural lectures, Africana collection (African Medical periodicals), a collection of CMUL postgraduate and undergraduate theses and simulation models.
  • There are about a total of half a thousand reading/viewing places in all the three floors of the Library building.
  • The first floor houses the Graduate Reading Hall, the Reference and Serial Collections. The Reference Desk on this floor provides help on everything from simple factual to detailed bibliographic information.
  • The second floor houses the Undergraduate Reading Hall, the Acquisitions, Cataloguing and Circulation Units. Several thousands of Reserve, Reference and Open shelf books are available on the floor.
  • The third floor houses the E-learning Resource Centre of the Library. It is well equipped with modern audio-visual and multimedia resources. The Centre acquires modern hardware and software that meet up with the demands of the information age. Also available are two seminar/multi-media rooms and a simulation room each with a sitting capacity for over thirty persons.

e-Learning

The following services and facilities are available in the E-Learning Centre of the Medical Library:

  • Free Internet Services
  • Free assistance on computerized literature search for research/project topics
  • Scholarly online research with free access to open and close access journals in academic databases such as SCIENCEDIRECT, SCOPUS, JSTOR, HINARI, JAYPEEDIGITAL, EBSCOHOST, PROJECT MUSE, WORLDCAT etc.
  • The first floor houses the Graduate Reading Hall, the Reference and Serial Collections. The Reference Desk on this floor provides help on everything from simple factual to detailed bibliographic information.
  • Conducive space for seminars, group discussions, workshops, lectures, practical computer training sessions etc.
  • Black & white and colour printout of document on A4 and A3 paper size.
  • Scanning and conversion of documents in various formats such as OCR, PDF, JPEG, etc.
  • Hands on training on computer and internet resources for self-development and enhancement of computer skills.

Catalogues

  • The Library operates two Catalogue schemes – Bernard and National Library of Medicine (NLM) (the most current one). The schemes are manually operated with card catalogue system available on the second floor. It holds the records of all the books and audio-visuals in the Library.
  • The books can be found under Author, Title and Subject arrangement in alphabetical order.

Membership

  • Membership with full privileges is open to
  • Members of the Court of Governors
  • Students and staff of CMUL
  • Associate membership is open to students, trainees, staff of the Faculties and institutes of the University of Lagos and any other person approved by the Medical Librarian or the Library Committee where necessary.
  • Temporary membership shall apply to persons admitted by the Medical Librarian on temporary basis.

Registration

  • Eligible members will be issued a Library Card after completing the necessary forms at the Circulation Desk.
  • CMUL/LUTH staff are required to obtain letters of introduction from their Heads of Department.
  • The Library Card should be kept safe at all times. Library users will be expected to show their Library Cards on request by any Library staff. Failure to produce the Library Card on request may deny a user of Library services.

Loss of Identification Card

  • The sum of ₦200.00 is payable for the replacement of lost Library Card. This amount is subject to review from time to time.

General Rules

  • All users leaving the library would be checked by the Porters on each floor and may also be searched by the Security Officials on the corridor leading to the main entrance of the Library.
  • Brief-cases, bags, umbrella, Lab Coats, waterproofs, etc., excluding valuables (money, jewelleries, etc.) must be deposited with the Porter at the Cloak Room or Porter’s Desk. A tally must be obtained for articles deposited. Readers should produce the tally to the Porter before articles/ item(s) can be collected
  • Users are required to be quiet in and around the Library and are expected to comport themselves properly in the library.
  • Food and beverages are not permitted in the library
  • Sleeping or committing any nuisance in the library is prohibited
  • Reservation of seats in the library is not allowed
  • Library closing will be announced twenty minutes before the doors are locked
  • Users are expected to complete all library transactions before closing time.
  • All acts of vandalism of library materials and equipment are prohibited.

The Medical Librarian may suspend or withdraw all privileges from any user who contravenes any of the Library Regulations and may refer the offender to the Library Committee for appropriate action

Borrowing

  • Books to be borrowed must be properly checked out at the Loans Desk. The safe custody and return of the books thereafter become the responsibility of the borrower. Loans transactions are to be done in person.
  • A user may, however, borrow through his agent provided the agent presents a duly signed request along with the borrower’s Library card and I.D. card for the loan.

Short Loan

  • Eligible members will be issued a Library Card after completing the necessary forms at the Circulation Desk.
  • Books in the Reference and Reserve Sections are to be used only within the Library.
  • However, books on reserve are to be borrowed for an initial period of two hours, renewable on hourly basis provided they are not needed by another user. A reserve book so renewed can be recalled at any time if needed by another user.
  • Current journals are not for loan and therefore cannot be taken out of the Library. Back issues may be borrowed over-night on request and approval by the Reference Librarian.

Loans Entitlement

  • All loans (except short loans) are renewable for the same period as the original loan unless requested for by another user.
  • Renewal may be made by presenting books physically at the Loans Desk or by post, written note, or by telephone.

Reservation

  • Any book on loan may be reserved by any library user. The user must complete a reservation form and will be notified as soon as the book is available.
  • A book will be reserved for three days from the date of the notification. Where the user fails to collect the book within the period, the reservation right will cease.
  • Borrowers wishing to be notified of the availability of a book reserved shall be required to pay the cost in advance.

Inter-Library Loans

  • Materials required by users and not available in the library may be obtained for the user through inter-library loan, both within and outside Nigeria. Fees will be charged for such services as appropriate.
  • Any user wishing to enjoy this facility will fill the appropriate form giving all details of the item required. The form should be handed to the officials at the Circulation /Reference Desk for necessary processing. The user will be informed when the required item is available for collection.
  • Copies of journal articles obtained through inter-Library loans are made available on request for consultation within the library.
  • Requestors, who wish to obtain photocopies of same, will fill the Photocopy request form obtainable at the Reference Desk and pay for the material to be photocopied.

Recall of library materials

  • A book renewed can be recalled at any time within the renewal period and should be returned immediately.
  • Any book recalled must be returned within three days of the date of recall.
  • Users should return all library materials before leaving Lagos for annual leave, semester vacation, and sabbatical/training Leave.

Overdue Books & Fines

  • Books must be returned promptly on or before the date due.
  • Notice will be sent: If books are not returned within six weeks after the notice such books will be deemed to be lost.
  • Reserve books kept overdue will attract fines of N00 per day
  • A borrower keeping any library book overdue or has any outstanding fines will be denied further loan until the books are renewed or returned and the fines settled accordingly.
  • Any user consistently keeping books overdue will be blacklisted with immediate effect and may be suspended as the Library Committee deems fit.

Damaged & Lost Books

Damaged books

  • The person responsible for the damage of a book will be required to pay an estimated cost of repairs not exceeding the current cost of the book.

Lost Books

  • Cost of replacement of a lost book will be calculated at the current price of the title plus a nominal charge to cover administrative costs.
  • The cost of replacement must be settled before the user is allowed to use the facilities of the library.
  • Fines cease to accrue on library materials reported lost. Material later found and returned will have fines re-imposed from the date due until return.
